My Last Semester

The last semester I was at school was Spring 2021. It was a lot like the other “zoom” semester. I would wake up, attend a lecture of ~100 people, camera off, and just listen to the professor lecture about some topic in computer science. I was strictly taking those classes at the time. Honestly, it was boring. Not being in person had a lot of impacts, I didn’t meet anyone that I didn’t already know, and I was less engaged in my classes than I usually was. Open note tests meant less studying and so forth. This did have the advantage of saving me a lot of time. The absence of having to walk to class, hanging out with roommates, etc. And so, I became career-focused.

After spending all of my days studying leetcode (a website for coding interviews) and practicing my answers to:

  • “How would you be a valuable asset to X company?"
  • "Give an example of a time where you led a group.”
  • And so forth...

I was fortunate enough to land an internship at Opendoor and TikTok. Now Opendoor was a company I worked for over the summer, my first real “job.” And that’s out of the scope of the prompt. Now TikTok, on the other hand, is where I spent my time for the Fall 2021 semester.

To say I was super excited was an understatement. I mean it was TikTok! My project was making a page that asked the user for their gender when they signed up for the app.

The actual page!

Now, I could talk all day about the implementation of that page:

  • The headaches of having hundreds user sign ups per second
  • The legal issues with asking people for their gender
  • Etc, etc...
But what I want to focus on are the life lessons this experience taught me. It made me scared of what life has to offer after college. With remote work being the norm for the foreseeable future, I found it hard to make friends at work. Relationships feel platonic - people seem to be nice because of work and how relationships can get you ahead. Time also flies when you are doing the same thing day in and day out. It’s on you to plan exciting trips and make new moves in life. No more having an auto-reset every 4 years, switching schools, and summer break - where you can make memories with friends and family without a care in the world. Lastly, it makes me sad to graduate. My time with 10,000+ similar-aged, young adults, or still “kids,” is almost over. I feel like I haven’t made the memories I wanted to. I mean, I missed out on the first semester back in person from COVID. As I looked at Snap stories, Instagram posts, I felt like I missed out. Truth is, no matter how much I learned from TikTok and how much it helped out my resume and initial career, I kind of regret it.
