ENGL149 Autobiography

Unprecedented Times

By: Aubry Dreikosen

In 2020 and 2021 the phrase ‘unprecedented times’ has been used to describe the current state of all our lives due to the COVID pandemic. I use the phrase ‘unprecedented times’ to describe the complete turnaround my life took last semester. Nothing crazy happened to me last semester, but it was in the mundane that the unprecedented happened.


I began work as a ULA for COMP311 last semester- the class that I had labeled ‘the bane of my existence’ just the semester prior. This meant I was now in charge of the education of 150 students, most of them my peers from high school or other classes. It was a hard balance to find between teaching the students and being their peer. Throughout the semester as I relearned the material so I could teach it I gained a greater appreciation for it and COMP311 may be my favorite class I’ve taken at UNC.



Over the summer I interned for a company called Hats and Ladders in New York. At the time this was the internship of my dreams. I started the semester ready to continue interning for them through the rest of college and had secured a job offer from them for post college. However, I wanted to explore my options, so I last minute signed up for a career fair. I met with GE about a hardware engineering internship – a field of computer science I promised myself I wouldn’t go into because both my parent work in that field and I was determined to be different. Now, I have accepted an internship with them in Schenectady, NY this summer- the city my mom grew up in- and am more excited about this job than my ‘dream job’.


Last semester I adopted a cat after swearing for many years I hated cats. I adopted a kitten named cheddar. As much as I would like to say cheddar and I lived happily ever after, that is not the case. As I brought cheddar to the animal shelter one morning for a surgery, he escaped from his carrier in the parking lot and ran away into the woods. I was devastated. My family, friends, and I spent weeks in the woods searching for him. A month after this whole fiasco, I adopted another cat named churro. Now he and I are inseparable, and I am happier than ever with an animal I swore I hated.



I now have a great job for the summer, am working with a professor right now as a ULA and have a pet that adores me. These are all things I wanted for myself and had hoped and dreamed would happen. However, the details are unprecedented for me, but I am proud of myself for venturing off the path I had created for myself. A detailed plan for life is good but it does not leave room for growth and change which is an ever-present part of life as I learned in the last semester.
