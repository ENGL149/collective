My Last Semester


Steve Wilmot

Sports Aspect

Last semester started off rough, with having to adjust to finally having in person classes along with contracting COVID, I was not off to the most ideal start. After to my not so ideal start, I slowly started to get adjusted to what the 'college life' was. Wake up, go to class, eat food, work out, repeat was my life until I got to the weekend. The weekend was what I was living for, with a combination of hanging out with friends, to watching UNC football, to watching NFL football. The weekend was the best time to relax and just be myself and I got to experience my 'ideal college life'. Getting to sporting events early has always been a passion of mine, and nothing changed as I would get to the stadium well before the game would start. üèàStarting the year with high hopes for UNC Football, I was soon let down as the team did not live up to the extremely high expectations. From the collective school spirit and the friends I made along the way, I would not trade those experiences for anything. The picture on your right is a picture taken after UNC beat Miami and was posted on the UNC football twitter. Another favorite moment of mine was when UNC defeated Wake Forest and the student section rushed the field after the victory. Along with watching sports, I also started to pick up some lesser-known sports as hobbies. I learned how to play both pool and foosball and would strive to improve each and every day competing against my friend. At the beginning of the semester, I could barely hit the ball straight but through constant practice I was not only able to improve my skills, but I helped my friend improve as well. This was one of the more enjoyable hobbies of mine, because it was outside of my comfort zone and something that I was not known for liking.

School Aspect

My classes on the other hand were not the most enjoyable, and I remember just thinking I had to make it to the weekend to survive. Having to learn the anatomy of the human body was the hardest class I had taken in my life, and it showed me what college was truly like. Constant nights staying awake till three in the morning just studying away just to repeat the process the next day. It took a real hit to my mental health because I had never experienced struggles in the classroom before that class. But as the semester went on, I was able to meet some other students in the class that helped me find study tips that worked for them. I was able to apply what they had previously taught me and make better grades on the upcoming tests. Through this class I was able to learn the necessity of study, as well as not going past the breaking point which will be helpful in my future classes. My other classes were not as difficult, but they still had valuable information to provide me with for my future endeavors. Through my experiences of last semester, I am hoping that I will continue to build along getting closer to my end goal of working with athletics of some sort after I graduate college.
