My Last Semester

Photo I took on October 1, 2021

Despite last semester being my third semester as a UNC student, it was only my first semester on campus. Yes, that is right, I am indeed a member of the infamous Class of 2020.
Having my entire first year of college virtually was obviously not the way I had pictured it; however, it meant that my anticipation and excitement
were at an all-time high for my first semester on campus. I had to walk around and find all my classrooms like a freshman, learn about different campus resources like a freshman,
and adjust to the overall college experience like a freshman. But I was not a freshman; and this knowledge motivated me to make the most of my time on campus
at Chapel Hill since I had already lost two out of my eight semesters.


The day I decided to attend Chapel Hill

Last semester, I did a nice job balancing schoolwork with extracurriculars and downtinme. I only took twelve credit hours because it was my first semester
on campus and I wanted to be able to get involved without bogging myself down in homework. My four courses were a combination of
general education requirements with major and minor specific courses that all piqued my interest without overwhelming me. I will say, the 8:00 AM
Art History was a little difficult, only because of the time of day. Rolling out of bed to go sit in a dark room was a risky choice on my part, but I did well overall.
In terms of grades, I had my best semester yet (I think learning in person was a large contribution to that), but it was the ways I was involved outside
of the classroom that truly made last semester special.


The piece from UNC's Ackland Art Museum I chose to write about for Art History.

I have danced since the age of two and never given it up. I danced competitively from age five all the way through my senior year of
high school and dance was something I wanted to have with me as a constant when I transitioned from high school to college. I joined a dance group at UNC
called Carolina Vibe and for my first year we danced completely over Zoom. Do not get me wrong, dancing over Zoom was fun, but it was in a room alone,
watching a laptop, instead of being in a studio with my friends. At the start of last semester, we met in person for the first time and I knew I had friends for life.
Everyone is so loving and supportive and I seriously could not have asked for a better group of friends. I loved talking about hard courses some of the older
girls had already taken, and meeting the new freshmen who were going to love the group as much as I did.
They gave me a sense of peace and community, even in such a chaotic time.


Carolina Vibe after our Fall Showcase on November 19, 2021

Carolina Vibe's Zoom Showcase in Spring 2021

My first REAL semester of college went so much better than I could have ever expected and it made me excited for the
rest of the memories I am going to make as a Carolina student.
I think dealing with a tough first year made me realize that I cannot take the rest of my time in college for granted.
