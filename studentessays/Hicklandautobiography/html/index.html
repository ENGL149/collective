An Autobiography of Justin Hickland’s Previous Semester




Fall 2021.

A systematic yet straightforward way of encapsulating my last semester, but my time was far from just a season and some numbers. Last semester was a beginning, a promise, and most importantly, an awakening. My name is Justin Hickland, and coming into my sophomore year of college, you might expect me to be filled with excitement and joy at returning to the campus. Instead, I was scared and uncertain as I was still recovering from almost two semesters of my college experience at home after being kicked off campus due to several COVID-19 outbreaks.

I came in fresh with a new dorm, new suitemates, and in-person classes. I was overwhelmed, to say the least, because I felt like I was just starting college even though I was no longer the newest student on campus. I stayed optimistic, and thankfully my semester began to take shape. I lived in a suite of eight guys on the second floor of Morrison residence hall. We were all quite different since we were randomly assigned to each other, but I couldn’t ask for better suitemates. We grew to know each other well throughout the semester over game nights, dinners, and my suitemate D’Andre’s strike system. We had quite a few laughs, and I feel comfortable with any of them at my side.

Another essential piece to know about me is that I am an undecided major. As a result, my classes are always quite random because I solely focus on completing general education courses, and last semester was no exception. I took French cuisine, intro to Africa, media and popular culture, beginner Spanish, and geology. The difference this semester is I was taking classes in person for the first time since the pandemic started. As crazy as all these classes sound, Spanish by far was the hardest. I don’t understand our society’s obsession with people learning a language later in life when the older we are, the harder it is to learn a language. It is not that I do not want to learn Spanish; I just do not believe it should be the most challenging class when I do not plan to use the language in my future career. Despite my struggles with Spanish, I found an interest in all of my other classes. My favorite by far was media and popular culture. This was the only class I chose out of my interests and not to meet a requirement. It was well worth it because I could utilize my talents and knowledge of media I enjoy, to contribute to class and make friends. The most surprising course I took was my French cuisine class. I had no prior experience in French cuisine but quickly fell in love with all the subtle implications food has in our everyday lives. My other courses definitely played a part in my semester, but they felt comparable to previous high school or online classes I had experienced before. Therefore, these classes offered differing experiences that influenced and shaped my academic and social interactions for a mostly ideal semester back on campus.

Overall, fall 2021 was a semester that started with uncertainty and left me with more questions than I came in with but ultimately set up the groundwork for the rest of my college experience.
